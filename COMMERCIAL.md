# Commercial License

Pharos is dual-licensed.

The default open-source license is the
[GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 only](LICENSE). It's
fine for self-hosting, internal use, and contributions to other
AGPL-compatible projects.

AGPL's network-use clause means that if you operate pharos as
part of a service offered to others (a hosted product, a managed
agent platform, an internal tool you ship to customers, etc.),
you must offer the corresponding source under the same license.
That's not always compatible with how a commercial product needs
to be distributed.

A commercial license is available for those cases. Under
commercial terms you receive:

- The right to embed pharos in a closed-source product without
  triggering AGPL's source-disclosure obligations
- The right to operate pharos inside a managed-service or
  sandbox-runner offering without releasing your service's
  source
- Priority on bug reports and feature requests for issues that
  block your deployment
- Direct support channel separate from the public issue tracker

- Preferred payment cadence (per-seat, per-deployment, flat
  annual)

## What we won't do

- Charge for non-commercial use. The AGPL covers that.
- Charge for internal tooling at companies where pharos doesn't
  touch external users. AGPL allows internal use freely.
- Require commercial customers to give up their own IP. The
  commercial license is a one-way grant from us to you for
  specific use cases — your code stays yours.
