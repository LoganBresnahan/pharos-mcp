**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD

## Context

What is the situation that demands a decision? What forces are at play — technical, organizational, philosophical? What constraints exist? What did we consider but rule out, and why?

This section is the most important. A reader six months from now will use it to judge whether the decision still applies or whether the underlying conditions have changed.

## Decision

What we are going to do, in one or two short paragraphs. Active voice. No hedging.

## Consequences

What becomes easier as a result. What becomes harder. What we now have to live with — including risks, follow-up work, and constraints this places on future decisions.

A good consequences section is honest about downsides. The point of an ADR is not to persuade — it is to record reality.
